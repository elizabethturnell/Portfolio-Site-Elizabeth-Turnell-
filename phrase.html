Language Learning App

Phrase

This language learning app focuses on learning sentences that are relevant when traveling, i.e., ‘where is the nearest bathroom?’ or ‘do you know how to get to the museum from here?’ The app centers around communication rather than learning a new language from the very beginning.

The Brief

I created a native language learning app that specifically focuses on tourists learning phrases they will most likely need to speak during their travel so they can communicate in the country’s native language.

Objective

Provide sentences that will prepare users and locals for their trip.

Problem

How can a tourist communicate in a country’s native language without having to learn a whole new language?

My role

UX/UI Designer from research to conception, visualization, and testing.

Timeline

May - Aug 2020

The Challenge:

Phrase will focus on teaching tourists basic, common phrases needed to travel when exploring a new city in a foreign country. The app will begin at teaching Spanish, French, German, and Japanese.

Understanding the User

User Personas and User Flow

After I conducted user interviews to understand what participants like and dislike about current language apps, I created a user persona to help visualize a person who has certain goals and motivations to learn key phrases before going on a trip.

“Leading up to my trip to Cuba I practiced my Spanish for 15-30 minutes a day for a month.” -David, 25, Chicago, IL

“I wish there was an app that could tell me how to order a glass of wine instead of just conjugations.” -Bethann, 51, Seattle, WA

I created a user persona to help me visualize a different perspective and see how someone would be motivated to use the Phrase app and how it would be beneficial for them based on personal goals and lifestyle.

User Flow

After creating a user persona I created a user flow so I could see not only why Stephanie wants to use Phrase but also how she might navigate the app. The user flow proved to be extremely helpful in visualizing the flow of the app and having a visual reference that prepared me for prototyping.

Prototyping

I wanted to sketch out prototyping on paper to visualize how wireframes would look based on the user flow I created above. I wanted to do a paper prototype just to throw together a quick sketch to help me comprehend the flow of the app.

Usability Testing

I wanted to conduct a usability test to see how users interact with the prototype and identify any flaws or frustrations they found while exploring the concept. My main takeaway from the testing was that I should simplify the functions already provided. Here is an example of how I simplified as function after receiving that feedback:

“If i’m clicking my flashcard i expect to see my flashcard and maybe i don’t want to click twice to see them.”

“I want exercises I will be using a lot to be accessible on the Home page.”

After usability testing i found that users didn’t want so many steps before they reach their exercise so I created tabs at the top of the screen for users to tap the category with the ability to create a new flashcard or create multiple flashcards.
